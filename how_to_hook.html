How To Hook

You need to know who you are hooking. Target audience is paramount to gaining a following. Audience encompasses more than just the groups which are intuitive to a piece, but also those who might be interested as well.

You need to know what to hook. Avoid simply using what is popular at a given time, as this could make a piece shallow. Add depth to your writing by offering details to things that might otherwise lack them, such as elements about a given object.

You need to know when and when not to hook. Too much of a good thing can be a bad thing. Ensure that you have just enough to draw someone in without saturating a piece.

You need to know where to hook. The start of a piece is important and generally should contain a hook there. Though beyond that, they should be placed sporadically throughout a piece. Though in doing this, they should not be placed in an easily discernable pattern, to keep the reader always guessing as to what happens next. They should be uncertain, but not lost.

You need to know why you are hooking to begin with. Without drawing in and capturing the reader, they may wander elsewhere. Though this goes beyond simply losing one reader. As people by nature gossip and that one reader can turn into two, and then those two become four. The reason for this is that gossip is exponential.
