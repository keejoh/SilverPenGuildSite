Narrative

Being able to write is one thing. Though knowing what to write is an altogether different beast. Fact or fiction, there needs to be something of a goal in mind. It does not need to be totally concrete, but it does require a certain concept in mind. This is because your piece needs to be driven towards something. Outline a rough idea of where that direction is going and then the rest should flow much more clearly.

Once the end goal is in mind, you need to figure out how you want to go about detailing the start and finish points. Starting the story is the most difficult part for many. The adage of “the first step is always the hardest” comes to mind. It is a statement that so often holds a truth for many people. Although, once the end goal is in mind, it makes it quite a bit easier to begin. What kind of foundations do the characters in the story have? What kind of basic equations make up the scientific formula? Essentially, how do you go from A to B?

Something to remember when crafting a narrative is that a story often revolves around more than one character, even if there are central characters in the form of a protagonist and antagonist. Bear in mind that these supporting characters have their own goals and aspirations too and that there is great value in exploring those strands. This is equally so for academic papers, as things related to your central topic can add great supporting information to grant a fuller presentation.
