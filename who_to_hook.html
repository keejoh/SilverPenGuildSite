Who To Hook

When considering what to hook, you must ask yourself what your audience is going to be. This is important, because audience is ultimately what generates revenue or interest. If you were to create a product that was based around science, but pitched it to Flat Earthers, then there is a higher chance of the product failing on the open market. This is because the Flat Earther movement omits most science-based evidence, in lieu of a faith-based system.

This topic is a bit more expansive than just knowing your target audience though. That is because if you were to pitch the science-based product, there may be others outside of the intuitive audiences, such as scientists, who might find interest in your product. This could be a school wanting an alternative to the common Pearson brand, science enthusiasts who window shop with new and interesting texts, or various interest groups. By allowing more than the typical audience have a chance to see your product in an ad or simply by posting in an online group that you would not normally post in, there may be a much larger audience generated as a result.
