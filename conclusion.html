Conclusion

Finishing a story is as important as the beginning. Often it is not what is in the middle of a story that people remember most. Instead, it is the start and finish which most sticks out in the mind. Due to this, the way in which you leave your audience is paramount to future success. Otherwise, you may find an audience that is distinctly underwhelmed. This is even more the case if the story buildup to the ending was fantastic, as it yields expectations that are ultimately unmet. These unmet expectations turn to contention and gossip, which may cost valuable customers later.

When finishing the story, determine if you are planning to continue it or not in a second installment or to bring the characters into another, unrelated story. If you are not wanting to continue the story and instead want it to be a one-off, then make sure the plots for the various supporting and main characters are tied up. Leaving plots hanging is one of the worst things to do as a content creator, as it affords no finality to the audience. This sense of wanting is a strong force of contention that can act as a persistent shadow on the content creator.

There is a partial exception to the leaving hanging story threads. That is when you want to create a second instalment or a series. Wrap up what is not useful or things you do not believe you will follow up on, but then leave open the things you may wish to expound upon later. This allows you to use the hanging thread to introduce the next installment, without having to consider the introduction so much.
